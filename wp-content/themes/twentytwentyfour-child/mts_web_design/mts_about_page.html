Our Sacred Story

About Marpa Translation Society

Continuing the noble tradition of the great translators, bringing authentic dharma wisdom across linguistic boundaries since 2015.

Our Sacred Journey

From the vision of one remarkable teacher to a global community of scholar-practitioners dedicated to preserving and sharing Buddhist wisdom.

The Vision

Having traveled extensively in both East and West, Drupon Khen Rinpoche Karma Lhabu recognized the critical need for authentic, accessible Buddhist translations. Observing that sincere practitioners often lack financial resources due to their dedication to practice rather than wealth accumulation, he envisioned a translation society operating on pure dharmic principles.

2015

Foundation at Thrangu Sekhar

The Marpa Translation Society was formally established at the Thrangu Sekhar Retreat Center in Nepal. Named after the great translator Marpa Lotsawa, who brought many essential tantric teachings from India to Tibet, our society committed to continuing this sacred tradition of making wisdom accessible across cultures.

2015

First Translation Projects

Our pioneering translators, all students of Rinpoche residing at the retreat center, began work on foundational texts. The principle was established: charge only production costs, never profit from dharma. Quality was paramount‚Äîeach translation refined through Rinpoche's teachings to ensure authentic transmission.

2016

Growing Community

Word spread among serious dharma practitioners worldwide. Our unique approach‚Äîcombining scholarly rigor with authentic practice experience‚Äîattracted dedicated translators fluent in multiple languages. The team expanded while maintaining our core principle: translators must be practitioners under Rinpoche's guidance.

2018

Global Impact

With translations available in English, Chinese, German, and French, our work reaches practitioners across continents. The MTS Knowledge Hub launches, creating a comprehensive digital library. Our cost-only model proves sustainable while keeping authentic dharma accessible to all sincere seekers.

2020

Continued Excellence

Today, with twenty-plus translators working on major projects, we continue Rinpoche's vision. Each text is refined during his teachings to retreatants, ensuring translations carry not just words but the living blessing of dharma. Our mission remains unchanged: authentic wisdom, accessible to all.

2025

Our Guiding Light

The vision and wisdom of Drupon Khen Rinpoche Karma Lhabu continues to inspire our mission of making authentic dharma accessible to all sincere practitioners.

Drupon Khen Rinpoche Karma Lhabu

Founder & Spiritual Director

A profound scholar and accomplished meditation master, Drupon Khen Rinpoche represents the authentic continuation of the Thrangu lineage. His extensive travels throughout Asia, Europe, and the Americas provided unique insights into the challenges facing dharma practitioners in different cultures.

Recognizing that many sincere practitioners dedicate their lives to spiritual development rather than material accumulation, Rinpoche established our cost-only principle. His deep understanding that "dharma is more than words" guides our translation methodology, ensuring each text carries the living essence of Buddhist wisdom.

At Thrangu Sekhar Retreat Center, Rinpoche personally oversees our translation work, refining each text through his teachings to ensure authentic transmission. His vision of translator-practitioners who combine scholarly excellence with genuine realization continues to shape our community.

"Someone who conveys the Dharma in their own language to their fellow countrymen is doing something wonderful and very important. With this motivation, I have been encouraging people to become translators."

Sacred Environment

Where Translation Meets Practice

Nestled in the serene mountains of Nepal, Thrangu Sekhar Retreat Center provides the ideal environment for our translation work. Here, surrounded by the natural beauty that has inspired spiritual seekers for millennia, our translators combine rigorous scholarly work with authentic dharma practice.

The center's unique atmosphere allows for the deep contemplation necessary for quality translation work. As Rinpoche teaches the texts to retreatants, our translations are continuously refined, ensuring they carry not just intellectual accuracy but the living blessing of transmitted wisdom.

This integration of practice and scholarship creates translations that serve practitioners at every level‚Äîfrom beginning students to advanced yogis seeking the most subtle nuances of meaning in classical Buddhist texts.

The mountain silence and community of practice provide the perfect conditions for translations that truly serve the dharma and its sincere practitioners worldwide.
