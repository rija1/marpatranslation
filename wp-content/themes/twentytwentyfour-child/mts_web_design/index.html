Making Tibetan Buddhism Accessible

Founded by Drupon Khen Rinpoche in 2015, the Marpa Translation Society is dedicated to translating the classics of Tibetan Buddhism for non-Tibetan-speaking practitioners worldwide, supporting their study and practice of the dharma.

Connect With Us

Our Sacred Mission

Following the vision of Drupon Khen Rinpoche, we continue the tradition of great translators like Marpa Lotsāwa, making profound Buddhist teachings accessible across linguistic boundaries.

"Having travelled widely in both the East and the West, I came to see the importance of good translators and translations. Many practitioners do not have a lot of money because they do not spend their time and effort making money, they practise. Therefore, it can be difficult for them to acquire texts."

"Someone who conveys the Dharma in their own language to their fellow countrymen, is doing something wonderful and very important. With this motivation, I have been encouraging some people to become translators."

— Drupon Khen Rinpoche Karma Lhabu, Founder

Cost-Only Approach

We charge only the equivalent of publishing costs, with no profit margin, making authentic dharma texts affordable for sincere practitioners.

Scholarly Collaboration

Our translators work closely under Rinpoche's guidance at Thrangu Sekhar Retreat Center, meeting with Khenpo Wangchuk to clarify difficult points.

Practitioner-Translators

As Rinpoche reminds us, "dharma is more than just words" - our translators are practitioners first, ensuring translations carry the feeling and blessing of the dharma.
